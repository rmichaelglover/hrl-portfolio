Gooblets & Zappies

a little world that grows itself · built with Elbie

A tiny 2-D world that grows and changes on its own. The land labels itself into biomes — desert, plains, forest, water — that drift and shift. The dumb little Gooblets munch the pink bushes and run to the water to drink… until the Zappies hunt them down. The Humans chop wood to build brown settlements they can hide in. And deep in the water lurk the Leviathans, who lunge at anyone drinking at the shore. Watch it all rise and fall forever. Click the world to play god.

What's happening. The land labels itself by relaxation — the same engine as the rest of Project Genesis. Every patch has four labels (water · desert · plains · forest) and each step it relaxes toward agreement with its neighbours, guided by a slowly drifting moisture map, so biomes form regions and then creep and change. Pink bushes sprout on the plains and forest and slowly ripen back after they're eaten.

The two species. Gooblets are dumb herbivores with two needs: they wander to the pink bushes to eat, and run to the water's edge to drink. Eat 5 bushes (with a drink in you) and a Gooblet makes a baby — but go too long without food or water and it drops. Zappies are hunters — they chase the nearest Gooblet and zap it (though a Gooblet often slips away, especially by darting to the water), and after zapping 5 Gooblets a Zappy makes a baby of its own. Too many Zappies and the Gooblets crash; then the Zappies starve and crash too, and the Gooblets bounce back — a boom-and-bust that never quite ends (a few new critters wander in from off-screen so the world never fully dies). A fun toy ecosystem, not real biology.

The Humans. Humans (dark red) also eat the pink bushes, but they gather wood from the forest and spend it to build brown settlement blocks. A Human standing on a settlement is safe from the Zappies (who hunt Humans too), and no other creature can walk onto settlement tiles. There can only be about two settlement blocks per Human — if there are ever too many (because Humans died off), the extra ones collapse back into plain land.

The Leviathans. Leviathans (dark blue) live in the ocean and never leave it. When a creature comes to drink at the shore, a nearby Leviathan swims over and eats it — unless it's a Human, who instead fishes the Leviathan and eats it (a big meal that fills both its hunger and its baby-meter!). A Leviathan makes a baby after eating 15 creatures. And they're territorial: whenever there are more than two, one hunts down another for a duel — a 50/50 fight, and the loser is gone. So Leviathans stay rare, and can even go extinct (drop more with the 🐋 tool).

The underground. Scattered dark holes drop any creature that steps on one into the caves below — hit 🕳️ Go Underground to watch. Down there are three biomes: mining grounds (grey), where Humans dig new tunnels; lava lands (purple-brown) with one big pool of lava that kills anything that touches it; and the underlake (brown) with water and glowing glowfruit to eat. You can't cross the black walls. Lurking in the tunnels are the Specters — pale cave predators that hunt whatever fell in and never breed, so as they die off the caves grow quiet. And every 500 days a giant black creature rises from the lava pool for a few seconds — any creature caught at the pool is turned into a fire minion that walks on lava unharmed, hunts the fallen, and (like the Specters) never breeds. While you're underground, the drop tools place creatures down in the caves — feed them to the dark, or grow a colony in the glowfruit lake.

The Humans get rich. As Humans mine, they collect stone and sometimes strike valuables. They carry it all up top: the stone becomes grey stone walls that keep the Zappies out, and the valuables go into the 💎 treasury. Once the treasury is full enough, a Human buys a boat and sails out onto the water to fish — where, if it's lucky, it might even hook a Leviathan.

The Sky Realm. Use the view button to rise to the ☁️ Sky Realm — a scatter of floating islands in the open sky (drop creatures & 👼 angels up here). The angels trade 1 💎 treasure for food, so a starving creature can run to one and eat. On the dark-red island lives a green dragon: gather 10 Humans on its island and they'll do battle — a 50/50 fight. Win and the Humans take a 👑 crown — one becomes King, and the others rally to protect him. Lose, and that whole group is gone. The islands are joined by bridges so creatures can cross between them. And back on the surface there's a purple portal square: have your Humans carry 3 treasures to it and it turns yellow — then any creature that steps on it is whisked up to the Sky Realm (and can step on the glowing sky portal to come back down).

Long live the King. A crowned King (he turns yellow) mostly wants one thing: to get home to the surface. Once down, his people build him a 4×4 hollow keep with a wheat farm around it, and 3 blue-robed servants shuttle wheat from the farm to feed him while he rules from inside his walls.

The four Monarchs. This world answers to four great Monarchs: the 🌊 Sea Monarch — a sea dragon whose folk raid Gooblets at the shore (but the folk must dodge the Leviathans, who leave the Sea Monarch alone); the 🐉 Dragon Monarch of the sky islands; the 👑 Human Monarch, the crowned King; and the 🔥 Fire Monarch, the Giant of the lava pool.

Play god. Pick a tool and click the map: drop 🔴 Gooblets, 🔵 Zappies, 🟥 Humans or 🐋 Leviathans (in water), 🌸 plant Bushes, or change the weather — 🌧️ Rain grows forest, ☀️ Drought spreads desert.
