Gooblets & Zappies

a little world that grows itself · built with Elbie

A tiny 2-D world that grows and changes on its own. The land labels itself into biomes — desert, plains, forest, water — that drift and shift. Out on the grass, the dumb little Gooblets graze… until the Zappies hunt them down. Watch the two populations rise and fall forever. Click the world to play god.

What's happening. The land labels itself by relaxation — the same engine as the rest of Project Genesis. Every patch has four possible labels (water · desert · plains · forest) and each step it relaxes toward agreement with its neighbours, guided by a slowly drifting moisture map. So biomes form coherent regions and then creep and change as the world "weather" wanders. Grass grows on plains and forest; graze it down and it takes time to grow back.

The two species. Gooblets are dumb herbivores — they wander toward grass, eat, and breed when they're full. Zappies are hunters — they chase the nearest Gooblet and zap it (though a Gooblet often slips away). Too many Zappies and the Gooblets crash; then the Zappies go hungry and crash too, and the Gooblets bounce back — a boom-and-bust that never quite ends (a few new critters wander in from off-screen so the world never fully dies). This is a toy ecosystem — a fun one — not a claim about real biology.

Play god. Pick a tool and click the map: drop 🔴 Gooblets or 🔵 Zappies, or make it 🌧️ Rain (grows forest) or bring a ☀️ Drought (spreads desert).
