The claim, made precise: energy is the pairing of an extensive quantity that counts (Diversity) with an intensive quantity that is shared (Unity).

E = ⟨λ, X⟩ = Σi λi Xi

λi = ∂E/∂Xi — Unity: the intensive conjugates. At equilibrium they are uniform — one shared value across every part. The zeroth law of thermodynamics is this: thermal equilibrium means all parts agree on one temperature. Unity is what makes the parts one system.

Where E = mc² actually sits

Naming m "diversity" and c² "unity" is an illuminating identification — real as structure, but a family resemblance, not a proof.
