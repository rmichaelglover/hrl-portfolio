Atlanta — the Great Water Cycle

The metro's commute reimagined as a hydrological cycle. Two monumental gyres circulate the city: an outward, clockwise Skywheel above and an inward, counter-clockwise Undertow below — breathing riders in to downtown each morning and out to the suburbs each evening.

How it's an HRL model. The metro is a grid of cells (the objects); each relaxes toward a circulation role — inward-conduit · outward-conduit · reservoir · congestion-eddy · off-network (noise) — under a compatibility that conserves flow, follows the demand gradient, and honours the two gyres' opposite winding directions (an orientation term — the same handedness the engine's order-3 factor sees). Riders are droplets advected by the blended gyre field; MARTA / WARTA / Cobblincan arms are fast conduits you paint onto the field, and Vroome jets fire express currents point-to-point. The congestion layer is a density field relaxed (neighbour-smoothed) each frame — the red eddies are where capacity is overrun. Where the gyres cross the real creeks (Chattahoochee, Peachtree Creek, South River), the figurative flow taps literal water.
