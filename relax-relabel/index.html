Relax, Relabel

Homer gets a new look, and a town that only ever read the surface assigns him a whole new self. A comedy about how a label is just a guess your neighbors stopped revising.

Couch gag — A barber's cape drops over each Simpson and they're restyled into wildly different "types," then snap back to normal. Maggie keeps the monocle.

Cold Open

INT. SPRINGFIELD NUCLEAR PLANT — DAY

Homer squints at a sign inches from his face: CAUTION — WET FLOOR. He cannot read it. He slips, kicking a barrel of something glowing green down the corridor. Klaxons.

DR. HIBBERT

(later, examining him) Homer, you need reading glasses. Constantly.

HOMER

(squinting at the diagnosis he can't read) D'oh-ptometry.

Act One

INT. SIMPSON KITCHEN — MORNING

Homer, in new glasses, looks — for the first time in his life — thoughtful. Marge is unsettled by how handsome contemplation is on him. At the plant, coworkers keep asking his opinion, which fills Homer with existential terror. He flees to the barber "to look even smarter."

INT. BARBER SHOP — DAY

JAKE THE BARBER, one eye on the game, shaves off Homer's three heroic combover hairs, absent-mindedly grooms his eyebrows into two magnificent expressive arches, and — reaching blind — straps a display-model bushy beard onto him. He spins the chair. Homer beholds the mirror.

Staring back: a soft-focus, suspiciously handsome man who looks like a cross between James Harden and LeBron James. A slight, glorious ambiguity.

HOMER

(low, amazed) Well, well, well. Look who reads now.

Act Two

EXT. SPRINGFIELD — CONTINUOUS

The town re-relaxes around him in real time. Lenny doesn't recognize him. Moe extends credit. Wiggum tips his cap with newfound respect. Nobody has changed one fact about Homer — only two eyebrows and a beard — and the whole label has flipped.

Outside a storefront studio — POSITIVE P POSSE RECORDS — a sharp, warm producer, DEX, looks up. He clocks Homer in half a second: the Duff stain, the peanut-butter thumbprint, the aura of a man who has never once been cool. He is not fooled. He just likes a genuine article.

DEX

Hey. I could use a guy like you.

HOMER

You can? Nobody can ever use me. Marge says I'm a "sunk cost."

DEX

(grinning) Nah, man. You're the realest thing that's walked in here all year. Come lay something down.

Homer becomes, in his own words, a "conscious rapper and studio gangster." The joke: every "hard" bar he attempts comes out impossibly wholesome. He's Pushin' P — except in Homer's mouth, P is Positivity (and Peanut butter). He answers questions, at random, in rhyme.

HOMER

(when Dex asks if the mic's on) The mic is on, the vibe is strong, we been here all along, ding-dong.

In the booth, eyes closed, dead sincere, Homer drops his breakout verse:

Marge, Marge — she's in charge /
if it were me, I'd be so large /
three hundred, fo' hundred, five hundred, six /
I'd be sittin' 'round eatin' peanut butter and hella twin Twix.

The booth goes silent. TREY wipes a tear.

TREY

That's… that's about his wife. And snacks. That's the most honest bar I ever heard in my life.

A soft-glow Young Thug homage flickers on the wall. Homer, misreading "Slime," salutes it with a jar of Skippy. The Posse are delighted by him — not because they believe the beard, but because his positivity is real and the whole town's shallowness is the funniest thing they've ever watched.

DEX

Man rebranded "Pushin' P" into a lifestyle and doesn't even know it.

Act Two-and-a-Half · Lisa

INT. SIMPSON KITCHEN — NIGHT

Lisa is thrilled, then troubled, then precise. This is the science, spoken at the table.

the joke's true engine LISA

Dad — nobody re-labeled you. They re-labeled two eyebrows and a beard. A label is just a guess your neighbors stopped revising. Change the surface, and the whole town relaxes to a different answer — it says everything about them, and nothing about you.

HOMER

(chewing) So… I contain multitudes?

LISA

(stunned) Did the glasses do that?

Act Three

INT. RECORDING STUDIO / EXT. BLOCK PARTY — NIGHT

A promoter demands "authentic street." Homer, incapable of a hard bar, instead ends a simmering beef between two crews by producing a fondue set and getting everyone to share. The Posse were never in it for the costume. When the display beard finally drops off mid-verse at the big show, the shallow town gasps and instantly relabels him back to "dumb Homer" — their gaze was always the superficial one. But Dex, Trey, and everyone who actually saw him keeps dancing, because what relaxed into truth was never the beard. It was the regard.

MARGE

(front row, mouthing his verse back to him) …peanut butter and hella twin Twix.

HOMER

(glasses fogged with joy) Turns out I was Homer the whole time. Positive P… it stands for Papa.

Tag

INT. MOE'S TAVERN — LATER

HOMER

They loved me for me, Moe.

MOE

They loved a beard, Homer.

HOMER

Same difference. Everybody's a beard for somethin'.

CUT TO: Lisa at home, writing in her notebook. PUSH IN on the page:

“autos = self. But the self only ever shows up in the mirror somebody else is holding.”

Maggie, still in the monocle, nods. SMASH TO CREDITS.
