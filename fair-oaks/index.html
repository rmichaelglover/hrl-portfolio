Fair Oaks

A nonprofit, accredited learning community, rooted at Fair Oaks Park — grown the way an oak grows: one ring at a time. A family of small schools that federate into a college, mature into a university, and widen into a lifelong institute for teaching, researching, learning, playing, and raising one another up.

Fair Oaks Park · 510 Bellemeade Dr SW · Marietta, GA 30008

The idea in one breath

You don't plant a university. You plant an acorn — a small, real school that works — and you let it add a ring each season. Each ring is a whole, self-sufficient thing before the next one starts. Nothing here requires the final form to exist before the first classroom opens.

The four rings — a growth path
I
Acorn
Years 0–3 · attainable now

A family of small Schools — the seeds

Open two to four micro-schools — small, multi-age academies (a dozen to a few dozen learners each), each with its own character but a shared ethos and a shared home at the park. This is the literal "different schools." Legally: a Georgia nonprofit with 501(c)(3) status; each school pursuing K–12 accreditation.

  • Prove one school works before adding the next — real families, real learning, real place.
  • Incorporate the nonprofit; seat a founding board; secure space at / beside Fair Oaks Park.
  • Begin candidacy with a K–12 accreditor (GISA, Cognia, SAIS, or GAC).
II
Sapling
Years 2–5 · the federation

Fair Oaks College — the schools, joined

The schools braid into one accredited institution — a collegium in the old sense: a community of schools under shared governance, a coherent K–12 program, one accreditation, one campus culture. The parts stay distinct; the whole becomes legible.

  • Achieve full K–12 accreditation; unify enrollment, calendar, and governance.
  • Establish an endowment seed and an income-adjusted tuition model.
  • Name note: "College" is used here in its classical, pre-degree sense — see the naming caveat below.
III
Oak
Years 5–10 · post-secondary

Fair Oaks University — teaching beyond childhood

Add grown-up learning: adult & community education, teacher formation, then certificates and — once accredited — degrees and research. This ring is earned, not declared, and it is the longest one.

  • Secure GNPEC authorization to operate post-secondary programs in Georgia (and to use "University" in the name).
  • Start with non-degree certificates & programs under GNPEC's permitted programming while pursuing accreditation.
  • Pursue SACSCOC (US-DOE-recognized) accreditation — the multi-year candidacy that unlocks degrees.
IV
Grove
Years 10+ · the mature form

Fair Oaks Institute — a grove, not a tree

The whole ecosystem, integrated cradle-to-elder: school, college, university, research labs, community programs, and open play — a self-sustaining grove rooted in the park, where the same people teach, research, learn, play, and raise the next ring up.

  • Research institutes & fellowships alongside the classrooms; town-and-gown as one.
  • Lifelong membership: the four-year-old and the ninety-year-old on the same grounds.
  • Steward the park itself as living campus and public good.

The scaffolding — what holds the tree up

Nonprofit foundation

Incorporate as a Georgia nonprofit corporation; adopt a mission & bylaws; seat a founding board; file for IRS 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status. Everything else hangs from this.

Accreditation pathway

  • K–12GISA / Cognia / SAIS / GAC — candidacy → full accreditation as the schools open.
  • OperateGNPEC authorization to run post-secondary programs in GA (and to use "College/University").
  • DegreesSACSCOC (US-DOE-recognized) accreditation — required before granting any degree in Georgia.

Campus & place

Anchored at Fair Oaks Park, 510 Bellemeade Dr SW, Marietta. Indoor rooms + the park itself as outdoor campus. Place is the curriculum, not just the setting.

Access & funding

Income-adjusted tuition, donors, grants, and an endowment seeded early. Nonprofit means mission-first: no family priced out of the first ring.

Governance & community

A board that holds the mission; families, neighbors, and park stewards who hold the day-to-day. The community is the institution.

The naming caveat

In Georgia you must clear the words "College" and "University" with GNPEC for post-secondary use, and you can't grant degrees without recognized accreditation. Use the names as aspiration & classical sense until each is earned.

What the Institute is for
TeachingResearchingLearningPlayingRaising up

Five verbs, one grove. The Institute isn't a building you graduate away from — it's a place you keep coming back to, at every age, to do all five. The four-year-old plays and learns; the researcher teaches and learns; everyone, always, is raising the next ring up.
