A melody is a labeling problem: give each beat a pitch so the whole line is mutually consistent — stepwise, in key, resting on the chord. So the same HRL engine that labels galaxies and genomes can compose. Press play. A tiny answer to Suno, on one engine.

How it composes. Sixteen beats (four bars of a I–V–vi–IV progression) are the objects; the eight scale pitches are the labels. A pairwise smoothness factor between neighbouring beats rewards stepwise motion and gentle leaps over static repeats and big jumps; a per-beat prior pulls each beat toward the chord tones of its bar, and the first and last beats are anchored to the tonic. The engine relaxes the whole line into agreement — a melody — which Web Audio then plays over soft chord pads. Every 🎲 Generate reseeds the prior, so you get a new tune each time. Honest MVP: symbolic, diatonic, one rhythm — but it's the real engine (hrl_generic), singing.
