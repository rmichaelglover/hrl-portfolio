Concept art · fictional / proposed · not an operating facility

St. James Park

A colorful civic-garden story world: water and welcome, contemplation and play, friendly beasts and careful stewardship. It is an imaginative design notebook—not a claim of ownership, endorsement, accreditation, medical service, or public access.

The grounds

A park imagined in living layers

Waters & earth

Michael's Lake, St. James Pond and Creek, Bellemeade Berms, Dunleith Dunes, playful ferries, pools, and color-coded gyres—all conceptual until engineering, environmental, accessibility, and safety review.

Garden & manger

A downstairs brick porch becomes a humble manger beneath a wooden deck. Hanging vines open toward an apple tree, roses, hawthorn, friendly animals, and an interfaith garden of reflection.

Play & fellowship

Skating, rolling, swimming, dancing, baseball heritage, and seasonal gathering share the ground. Institutional teams are possible invited partners only after written agreements—never implied endorsements.

Voxel future

An original block-built simulation can explore paths, water, accessibility, and stories before physical design. It will use original assets and clearly distinguish fantasy systems from buildable infrastructure.

The mushroom wetlands

Fungi between water and woodland

Storybook mushrooms are interspersed along shaded wetland edges, fallen logs, berm toes, and raised paths—not in swimming water or accessible travel lanes. Chanterelle-gold, oyster-white, chestnut, lion's-mane, wood-ear, and bracket-like forms provide visual inspiration only. No wild mushroom is represented as edible: real planting or foraging would require ecological review and expert identification.

Safety zoning

Natural edges, unmistakable boundaries

Native hedges, woven willow, planted berms, split timber rails, thorn-free living screens, and rain gardens soften boundaries and guide movement. They supplement—not replace—engineered, code-compliant barriers around pools, moving water, machinery, steep drops, service roads, and other hazards.

● Green · WelcomeOpen public paths, gardens, gathering lawns, and accessible routes.
▲ Amber · CautionWet ground, slopes, crossings, activity edges, and supervised areas.
■ Red · RestrictedDanger, machinery, deep water, utilities, construction, and staff-only zones.
✚ Blue · AssistanceFirst aid, emergency communication, accessible refuge, and staffed help points.

Every marker pairs color with plain words, a distinct shape or symbol, directional arrows where needed, high contrast, reflective/night treatment, tactile or raised information where appropriate, and multilingual or pictorial guidance. Final placement requires an accessibility and professional safety review.

Fictional character charter

Stewards of care and imagination

St. James

Supervising groundskeeper, watcher of the waters, ceremonial caretaker of departed good souls, friend to the friendless, and mentor or brother to those seeking fellowship. He is a future fictional robot character—not security, lifeguard, clinician, or clergy.

The Rose of Hawthorne

Rose Ellen Nettles Glover is privately noted as one inspiration for the fictional Green Queens. The Rose presides symbolically as Mistress of the Lands; the title conveys no legal authority or public endorsement.

First Aid Shepherd

A daring blue-haired punk-rock climber character coordinates visible first-aid wayfinding. Any real nursing, lifeguarding, rescue, or clinical work requires consent, credentials, staffing, and defined scope.

Woodland court

The Pine Tree Prince, Cream Queen, Photons, and Nhotons bring trees, gentle farm lore, color, and cosmic play. Real people remain private unless they knowingly approve a public fictionalization.

Responsible boundaries

Care requires real qualifications

Story titles do not create authority. A real wellness facility, insurer, pharmacy, dairy, helipad, healthcare database, or clinical program would require appropriate licenses, governance, insurance, accessibility, privacy controls, safeguarding, and professional supervision. “St. James Real Health Data Systems,” “St. Nate's,” and related names remain concepts and must not collect protected health information, sell coverage, dispense regulated products, or promise treatment. HIPAA compliance cannot be guaranteed by a title; it requires continuing legal, technical, clinical, and organizational work.
Privacy covenant

Publicly welcoming; privately careful

The public story omits residential addresses, occupancy, entrances, egress details, security information, private health data, and unapproved likenesses. Named real people are inspirations or proposed participants only—not employees, owners, hosts, partners, saints, clinicians, or endorsers without their informed consent.
